### **2024 Batch: WITHDRAWN** ‚ùå

**Timeline:**
- **August 17, 2024:** UPSC issues advertisement for **45 positions**
- **August 20, 2024:** Advertisement **WITHDRAWN** (within 3 days!)

**Positions Advertised:**
- Levels: Joint Secretary, Director, Deputy Secretary
- Departments: Commerce, Agriculture, Financial Services, Revenue, multiple others

**Withdrawal Reasons:**
1. **No Reservation Provisions** for SC/ST/OBC/EWS communities
2. **Opposition Criticism** - Called "systematic attack on Dalits, OBCs, Adivasis"
3. **Transparency Concerns** in selection process
4. **Political Backlash** from opposition and some NDA allies

**Political Reactions:**
- **Rahul Gandhi (Congress):** Called it attack on social justice
- **RJD, TMC, DMK:** Strongly opposed
- **Concerns:** RSS ideology being installed in bureaucracy

**Government Response:**
- Minister Jitendra Singh requested UPSC to withdraw
- Cited PM Modi's "absolute commitment to social justice"
- Promised revised framework with reservation provisions

**Current Status:** Policy under review, no timeline for reissue

## Conclusion

The parallel research using multiple agents has revealed that **India's lateral entry scheme is far more limited than originally understood**:

**KEY TAKEAWAYS:**
1. **Only 10 appointments in 6 years** (2018-2024)
2. **One confirmed exit** (Amber Dubey in 2022) due to systemic challenges
3. **Two-year pause** (2022-2023) with no activity
4. **2024 revival failed** within 3 days due to political opposition
5. **Most appointees invisible** in public domain (by choice or protocol)
6. **One success story** (Amber Dubey) with documented achievements
7. **Systemic resistance** from career IAS officers documented
8. **Future uncertain** - no timeline for scheme revival

The scheme remains **politically controversial** and **operationally challenging**, with the 2024 withdrawal showing that fundamental issues around **reservation provisions and transparency** have not been resolved.
