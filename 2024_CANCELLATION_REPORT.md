# 2024 Lateral Entry Cancellation: Complete Report

## Executive Summary
**Zero appointments were made in 2024.** Advertisement No. 54/2024 for 45 senior positions was cancelled within 72 hours of publication due to a major controversy over the absence of reservation policies for SC/ST/OBC candidates.

## Key Findings

### Advertisement Details
- **Advertisement Number:** 54/2024
- **Published:** August 17, 2024 (UPSC Employment News, newspapers, website)
- **Cancelled:** August 20, 2024 (72 hours later)
- **Positions Advertised:** 45 total
  - 10 Joint Secretary positions
  - 35 Director/Deputy Secretary positions

### The 72-Hour Timeline

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| **Aug 17, 2024** | UPSC publishes Advertisement No. 54/2024 |
| **Aug 17-19, 2024** | Opposition parties (Congress, SP, BSP) and NDA allies (LJP, JD(U)) protest absence of SC/ST/OBC reservations |
| **Aug 20, 2024** | Minister Jitendra Singh writes to UPSC requesting cancellation |
| **Aug 20, 2024** | UPSC officially cancels advertisement |

## Why It Was Cancelled

### Core Issue: Reservation Policy
The advertisement did not include quotas for SC/ST/OBC candidates, which triggered:
1. **Political Opposition:** Congress, SP, BSP strongly protested
2. **Allied Pressure:** NDA allies (LJP, JD(U)) also objected
3. **Constitutional Concerns:** Critics argued it bypassed Article 16(4) of the Constitution

### Government's Legal Position
- Positions classified as "single-cadre posts" under Supreme Court precedent (PGIMER case)
- Reservation technically not applicable to such posts
- However, politically untenable and required comprehensive review

### Government Response
Minister Jitendra Singh stated:
> "The Prime Minister is of the firm belief that the process of lateral entry must be aligned with the principles of equity and social justice enshrined in our Constitution, particularly concerning the provisions of reservation."

## Forward-Looking Analysis

### Three Policy Scenarios

1. **Incorporate Reservation**
   - Develop mechanism to apply SC/ST/OBC quotas
   - **Risk:** Complicates specialized talent search, potential legal challenges

2. **Redefine 'Single-Post' Exemption**
   - Strengthen legal justification
   - **Risk:** Politically explosive, invites further backlash

3. **Scrap Lateral Entry**
   - Abandon policy due to complexity
   - **Risk:** Permanent loss of domain expertise channel

### What to Watch in 2025

Key indicators that policy is being revived:
1. **DoPT Office Memorandum:** New policy guidance on reservation
2. **Parliamentary Committee Report:** Findings from ongoing review
3. **New Advertisement:** Will require robust constitutional basis
4. **Language on Quotas:** Any future advertisement must address reservation explicitly
